🔑 KEY INSIGHTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Why Current Design Feels Like Ecommerce:
  1. Image too small (160px) - product icon size
  2. Discount badges "-20%" - retail pattern
  3. Generic category labels - not food context
  4. Requires page load to add - friction
  5. No delivery/dietary signals - missing food info

What Food Apps Do Differently:
  1. Large images (200px+) - food dominates
  2. "Chef's Pick" tags - quality, not discount
  3. Restaurant context - credibility
  4. Direct add button - frictionless
  5. Delivery time visible - urgency signal

Food Ordering Psychology:
  • Emotional decision first ("Do I want this?")
  • Speed matters (hungry users want quick satisfaction)
  • Visual first (<2 second scan to decide)
  • Practical second ("Can I get it? How long?")
  • Impulse driven (low friction = higher AOV)
